## Population

- 631 patients evaluated, **201 randomised**
- 100 active treatment (67 propranolol, 33 carvedilol), 101 placebo
- Inclusion: compensated cirrhosis with CSPH (HVPG >= 10 mmHg) without high-risk varices
- Responders (HVPG decrease >= 10%) received propranolol (up to 160 mg BID); non-responders received carvedilol (up to 25 mg/day)

## Primary Endpoint

Incidence of cirrhosis decompensation (ascites, bleeding, or overt encephalopathy) or death.
ITT analysis with deaths unrelated to liver as competing events.

## Effect Size

| Outcome | Beta-blockers | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---------|--------------|---------|-------------|---|
| **Primary (decompensation or death)** | 16/100 (16%) | 27/101 (27%) | **0.51 (0.26--0.97)** | 0.041 |
| Ascites (driver of effect) | -- | -- | **0.44 (0.20--0.97)** | 0.030 |

## Key Conclusions

- Beta-blockers increased decompensation-free survival in compensated cirrhosis with CSPH
- Benefit driven primarily by reduced incidence of ascites
- Adverse event profile similar between groups (6 severe AEs, 4 in beta-blocker group)
- Funded by Spanish Ministries of Health and Economy
