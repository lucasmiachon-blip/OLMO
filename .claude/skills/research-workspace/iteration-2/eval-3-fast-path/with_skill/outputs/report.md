## Study Profile

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| **Design** | Multicenter RCT, double-blind, placebo-controlled |
| **Registry** | NCT01059396 |
| **Sites** | 8 hospitals, Spain |
| **Enrollment** | 631 screened, 201 randomized |
| **Arms** | Beta-blocker (n=100): propranolol (n=67) or carvedilol (n=33) vs Placebo (n=101) |
| **Population** | Compensated cirrhosis with CSPH (HVPG >= 10 mmHg), without high-risk varices |
| **Drug selection** | Propranolol for HVPG-responders (decrease >= 10%); carvedilol for non-responders |
| **Primary endpoint** | Cirrhosis decompensation (ascites, bleeding, or overt encephalopathy) or death |
| **Follow-up** | Median not stated in abstract; enrollment Jan 2010 to Jul 2013 |

## Primary Results

| Outcome | Beta-blocker | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---------|-------------|---------|-------------|---|
| **Decompensation or death** | 16/100 (16%) | 27/101 (27%) | **0.51 (0.26-0.97)** | 0.041 |
| Ascites (driver) | — | — | **0.44 (0.20-0.97)** | 0.030 |

- ARR = 11% (27% - 16%)
- NNT ~ 9 (1/0.11) [calculated from event rates; CI not provided in abstract]
- ITT analysis treating non-liver deaths as competing events

## Safety

Overall adverse event incidence similar between groups. Six severe adverse events (4 in beta-blocker arm).

## Clinical Significance

PREDESCI is the landmark RCT demonstrating that beta-blockers prevent first decompensation in compensated cirrhosis with CSPH. The benefit was driven primarily by ascites prevention. This trial is the cornerstone evidence for the Baveno VII recommendation to treat CSPH with NSBB even in absence of high-risk varices.
