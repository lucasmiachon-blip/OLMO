## Primary Endpoint

Incidence of cirrhosis decompensation (ascites, bleeding, or overt encephalopathy) or death.

Analysis: ITT with deaths unrelated to liver treated as competing events.

## Key Results

| Outcome | Beta-blockers | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p |
|---------|---------------|---------|--------------|---|
| **Primary (decompensation or death)** | 16/100 (16%) | 27/101 (27%) | **0.51 (0.26--0.97)** | 0.041 |
| Ascites (driver) | -- | -- | **0.44 (0.20--0.97)** | 0.030 |

- The benefit was driven primarily by reduced incidence of ascites.
- Overall adverse event incidence was similar between groups.
- 6 severe adverse events (4 in beta-blocker group).

## Clinical Significance

First RCT demonstrating that beta-blockers increase decompensation-free survival in compensated cirrhosis with CSPH. The effect was mainly through prevention of ascites. This trial is foundational for BAVENO VII recommendations on early beta-blocker use in CSPH.
