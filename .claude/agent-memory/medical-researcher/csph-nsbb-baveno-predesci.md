## Key BAVENO VII Statements (PMID 35120736)

### Statement 2.16 (Changed, B.1)
"In patients with virus- and/or alcohol-related cACLD and non-obese (BMI <30 kg/m2) NASH-related cACLD, a LSM value by TE of >=25 kPa is sufficient to rule in CSPH (specificity and positive predictive value >90%)"

### Statement 5.15 (Changed)
"Carvedilol is the preferred NSBB in compensated cirrhosis, since it is more effective at reducing HVPG (A.1), has a tendency towards greater benefit in preventing decompensation and towards better tolerance than traditional NSBBs and has been demonstrated to improve survival (B.1)"

### Statement 5.17 (New, B.2)
"Patients with compensated cirrhosis who are on NSBBs for the prevention of decompensation do not need a screening endoscopy for the detection of varices since endoscopy will not change management."

## PREDESCI Key Data (PMID 30910320)

- n=201 (100 NSBB, 101 placebo)
- Population: compensated cirrhosis + CSPH (HVPG >=10) WITHOUT high-risk varices
- NSBB: propranolol (n=67, HVPG responders) + carvedilol (n=33, non-responders)
- Etiology: HCV 54-58%, alcohol 14-19%, HCV+alcohol 8-9%, NASH 5-8%
- Median follow-up: 37 months (IQR 27-47)
- Primary endpoint: 16% vs 27% → ARR 11% → NNT 9 (95% CI ~5-infinity)
- HR 0.51 (95% CI 0.26-0.97), p=0.041
- Driven by ascites: HR 0.44 (95% CI 0.20-0.97)
- Subgroup: benefit "consistent across subgroups," MORE pronounced in non-alcoholic cirrhosis
- Carvedilol dosing in PREDESCI: up to 25 mg/day (non-responders to propranolol)

## Alcohol-Specific LSM Caveats

1. Active drinking overestimates LSM by ~2.6 kPa at 4 weeks (Gianni 2017)
2. Abstinent patients: median -4.9 kPa drop over ~32.5 weeks (Bardou-Jacquet 2013)
3. Even during/after withdrawal: LSM >=25 kPa has PPV 93% for cACLD (Legros 2022)
4. EASL 2021 (PMID 34166721): defer TE 2-4 weeks post alcohol cessation
5. KEY: cACLD validation (Legros) is NOT the same as CSPH validation. PPV for CSPH may differ.

## Carvedilol Dosing

- BAVENO VII: 6.25-12.5 mg/day
- AASLD 2024: 12.5 mg/day (target)
- Turco 2023: "target dose of carvedilol to treat PH should be 12.5 mg/day"
- Clinical practice: start 6.25 mg/day, titrate to 12.5 mg/day
- Monitor: SBP >90 mmHg, HR >50 bpm
