# SR vs MA vs Umbrella Review — Evidence for Slide

Researched: 2026-04-02

## Key Quantitative Data (all VERIFIED)

### Publication volume
- ~80 SRs/day in 2019 (up from ~4/day in 2000). 20x increase in 20 years. Hoffmann 2021, PMID 34091022.
- 2000: 1,432 SRs indexed. 2019: 29,073 SRs indexed.
- 58.9% of SRs include meta-analysis (overall). China: 94.9%.
- SRs growth 2,728% (1991-2014) vs 153% total PubMed. Ioannidis 2016, PMID 27620683.

### Proportion SR with/without MA
- Hoffmann 2021: 58.9% overall include MA (PMID 34091022)
- Page 2016: 63% include MA (PMID 27218655)
- ~37-41% of SRs do NOT include meta-analysis

### Umbrella reviews growth
- 2,965 publications identified (WoS, 2010-2023). Nayak 2025, PMID 40212143.
- Since 2016: >100/year. Peak 2023: >700 papers.
- Term coined by JBI. Cochrane calls them "overviews of reviews."

### Terminology confusion
- Cochrane reviews only named themselves "systematic review" in title 22.6% of the time (vs 73.4% non-Cochrane). Hoffmann 2021.
- PubMed tags inaccurate: ~2,500 stringent SRs in 2004, but PubMed tag returned 8,989. Ioannidis 2016.

## Definitions (canonical sources)

### Systematic Review — Cochrane Handbook v6.5, Ch.1, s1.1
"A systematic review attempts to collate all the empirical evidence that fits pre-specified eligibility criteria in order to answer a specific research question."

### Meta-Analysis — Cochrane Handbook v6.5, Ch.10, s10.2
"The statistical combination of results from two or more separate studies."
Also: "Meta-analysis yields an overall statistic (together with its CI) that summarizes the effectiveness of an experimental intervention compared with a comparator."

### Overview of Reviews / Umbrella Review — Cochrane Handbook v6.5, Ch.V, s.V.2.1
"Explicit and systematic methods to search for and identify multiple systematic reviews on related research questions in the same topic area."
Unit of analysis = systematic reviews (not primary studies).
Also called: umbrella review, review of reviews, meta-review.

### Glass 1976 (coined "meta-analysis")
"Meta-analysis refers to the analysis of analyses." Glass GV. Primary, Secondary, and Meta-Analysis of Research. Educational Researcher 1976;5(10):3-8. DOI: 10.3102/0013189X005010003. No PMID (not PubMed-indexed).
